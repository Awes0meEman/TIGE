The following idea for some kind of auto battler MMO with base building, leveling/skilling, and auto battler or possibly roguelike elements to it. Builds can revolve around items/stats that the player has trained. 

# Inspiration
Oldschool Runescape for sure. Definitely want a leveling system and in depth crafting system that feels meaningful.

Auto battlers like He Is Coming. 

I want to think about this more and how it'll be implemented, but the player may possibly be able to use items like potions and abilities. 

Base management. This comes from a lot of games but I think the idea of having a more or less set base that can be upgraded through training/rewards is ideal. Think like Heroes of Hammerwatch 2 base system, or Fort Forinthry from RS3.

I kind of want to think about having the game be a little bit meta, like the game knows that it's a game. A little bit of a Dungeon Crawler Carl vibe, but just the slightest. Maybe the tooltips make snarky quips here and there or something.

# Skills
>[!info] Skill Ideas
> > [!summary]- Crafting skills.
  >>>[!tip] Should have broken down subskills for different things. I think if we set out to keep each skill "mirrored" in the way that milestones are available at the same level and leveling is consistent between skills. It could be cool to call these jobs instead of skills, but that might just be too much FFXIV.
>>- Blacksmithing: making items out of metal
>>- Fletching: making arrows and bows
>>- Carpentry: making building materials out of wood
>>- Masonry: making building materials out of stone
>>- Farming: growing crops for food and potion ingredients
>>- Cooking: cooking food with various ingredients
>>- Husbandry: raising various animals, livestock or other
>>- Goldsmithing: making jewelry out of various precious metals and gems
>>- Leatherworking: tanning and crafting items out of various creature hides
>>- Shipwright: building ships, boats, and the like
>>- Fishing: it's not a real MMO until you can fish in it
>
>>[!summary]- Magic Skills
>>>[!tip] Should have a couple of magic skills. I think these should be "expensive" to train, requiring rare materials from powerful creatures. 
>> - Enchanting: making magical items 
>>- Herbalism: making potions
>> - Incantations: your general quickly cast spells like fireball or something like that
>> - Rituals: spells that require preparation and time to cast, like raise dead
>
>>[!summary]- Combat Skills
>>>[!tip] Should be related to combat and fighting. These skills will determine both how much damage you deal and how much damage you take from a respective form of combat. Magic used in combat is going to be separately dictated by the magic skills, as magic in combat will be very different from normal attacks. You cannot reduce magical damage through skills, but certain spells or magical effects will work to counteract other spells.
>>- Melee: close quarters combat with traditional weapons. Training this skill increases damage dealt with melee weapons and reduces damage taken from melee attacks
>>- Archery: training with long range weapons like bows and crossbows. Training this skill increases damage dealt with ranged weapons and reduces damage taken from ranged weapons
>>- Artillery: training with heavy weaponry like siege engines and explosives. Training in this skill increases damage dealt with artillery weapons and explosives.
